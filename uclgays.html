PrEP Accessibility at UCLA

About

After identifying a crucial concern of sexual health safety within the queer community, particularly in relation to HIV and college campuses, we moved forward with our website to introduce a platform that empowers queer undergraduate UCLA students by creating a space for open, honest discussion of the struggles to obtain Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis, the HIV-preventative medication otherwise known as PrEP. Our website focuses on showcasing why people take PrEP, the struggles to obtain or learn about PrEP, and thereby see whether the existing sexual health infrastructure regarding PrEP at UCLA satisfies the queer undergrraduate community. 

For this project, I was in charge of the web design. I worked closely with the back-end developer to create a layout of the website that not only is intuitive, but communicative and enfocuses on the user's stories. I first started with making wireframes for our group website, focusing mainly on including the requirements for our class, but also a way to highlight and centralize our user stories in a way that was not too overwhelming and user-friendly. We wanted to display answers to our Google Survey using GIS, JavaScript, and HTML/CSS that will be user-friendly, highlight the importance and voices of these community members, and lead us to our main goal which is to be descriptive, rather than prescriptive, allies towards this community. 

First Version

 

While our first version included what was required, it was missing the most vital thing which was a clear and easy-to-follow narrative for our mission with this project. To emphasize this, we revisited our flow chart to hone in on the narrative we are trying to vocalize, and revisioned our prototypes. 

In addition to revising our flow chart, we met up multiple times and discussed heavily with our instructor, Albert, who worked through the process and really gave us the foundation to move forward with our vision. During these discussions, I made wireframes and diagrams to possible ideas that eventually led us to our final version. 

After several revisions and edits, we finally settled on a layout that we found empowered our user's stories, be accessible and user-friendly.
